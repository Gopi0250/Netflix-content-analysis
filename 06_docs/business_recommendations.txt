EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

PROJECT: Netflix Content Strategy & Viewership Analysis
ANALYST: Gopi
DATE: 15-6-26
DATASET: 8,804 Netflix titles across 88 countries (1925–2021)

OBJECTIVE:
Analyze Netflix's content catalog to identify strategic patterns
and deliver data-driven recommendations to the content team.

KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY:
✅ Netflix is 70% Movies, 30% TV Shows
✅ USA dominates with 42% of all content
✅ International Movies & Dramas are top genres
✅ 67% of content is Library (older) content
✅ December & July are peak content addition months
✅ TV-MA (Adult) content dominates at 38%
✅ 67% of TV Shows have only 1 season
✅ Average movie duration is ~100 minutes
✅ Content additions peaked in 2019 (2,153 titles)
✅ India is #2 content producer with 972 titles

5 BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS

RECOMMENDATION 1 — Invest More in TV Show Production
FINDING:
Only 30% of Netflix catalog is TV Shows
Yet TV Shows drive higher engagement and longer watch time
67% of TV Shows have only 1 season — most are cancelled early

BUSINESS INSIGHT:
Netflix is heavily movie-focused but streaming platforms
globally are winning with serialized TV content.
Competitors like Amazon Prime and Disney+ are investing
heavily in multi-season TV series.

RECOMMENDATION:
Increase TV Show investment from 30% to 45% of new content budget
Focus on 2-3 season commitments instead of single seasons
This reduces churn and increases subscriber retention

EXPECTED IMPACT:
Higher average watch time per subscriber
Reduced monthly churn rate
Stronger subscriber loyalty


RECOMMENDATION 2 — Expand International Content Beyond USA
FINDING:
USA produces 42% of all Netflix content
India is #2 with only 972 titles (11%)
88 countries represented but top 3 dominate 65% of catalog

BUSINESS INSIGHT:
Netflix's fastest growing subscriber base is in
Asia-Pacific and Latin America markets.
Local language content drives local subscriptions.
The success of Money Heist (Spain) and Squid Game (Korea)
proves international content has global appeal.

RECOMMENDATION:
Increase content acquisition from Southeast Asia
(Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam) by 40%
Invest in Korean, Turkish, and Nigerian original productions
Target: Reduce USA content dominance from 42% to 30%

EXPECTED IMPACT:
Access to 500M+ new potential subscribers
Reduced content production costs (international = cheaper)
Global cultural diversity appeals to all subscriber segments


RECOMMENDATION 3 — Rebalance Content Rating Strategy
FINDING:
38% of content is TV-MA (Adult only)
Only 12% is Family/Kids content (TV-G, TV-PG, G, PG)
Family accounts represent 2-3 subscribers per account

BUSINESS INSIGHT:
Family plan subscribers watch more content per account
Kids content creates habitual daily viewing patterns
Disney+ dominates family segment — Netflix is losing here

RECOMMENDATION:
Increase Family and Kids content from 12% to 25%
Create more PG and TV-PG rated original content
Develop animated series targeting 6-12 age group

EXPECTED IMPACT:
Higher account-level revenue (family plans cost more)
Reduced churn (families stay subscribed for kids)
Direct competition with Disney+ in family segment


RECOMMENDATION 4 — Optimize Content Release Timing
FINDING:
December highest content additions (holiday season)
July second highest (summer)
February lowest content additions
Content drops are inconsistent month to month

BUSINESS INSIGHT:
Subscribers cancel when there is no new content to watch
February and May show significant content drought
Consistent monthly releases maintain subscriber engagement

RECOMMENDATION:
Implement monthly content release quotas — minimum 400 titles/month
Increase February releases by 35% (currently lowest)
Schedule major original releases for Q1 (Jan-Mar)
to counter post-holiday subscription cancellations

EXPECTED IMPACT:
Reduced seasonal churn spikes
More predictable subscriber engagement
Better content marketing planning

RECOMMENDATION 5 — Convert Library Content to Fresh Originals
FINDING:
67% of Netflix content is Library content
(added more than 1 year after original release)
Only 33% is Fresh content
(added within 1 year of release)

BUSINESS INSIGHT:
Library content is cheap to acquire but has low
discovery rates — subscribers have often seen it elsewhere
Netflix Originals (fresh content) drive new subscriptions
and press coverage that organic content cannot

RECOMMENDATION:
Shift content acquisition budget:
Current:  70% Library + 30% Originals
Target:   45% Library + 55% Originals

Focus originals on underrepresented genres:
Documentary, Stand-up Comedy, Anime

EXPECTED IMPACT:
Higher new subscriber acquisition through original buzz
Premium brand positioning vs competitors
Press coverage worth millions in free marketing
